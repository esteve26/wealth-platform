# 1. Purpose

Document Management is responsible for managing every business document associated with a Workspace.

Documents may be uploaded by users, imported from external providers or generated by the platform.

The capability manages documents throughout their entire lifecycle while preserving integrity, traceability and access control.

---

# 2. Responsibilities

Responsible for:

* document lifecycle;
* document storage;
* document metadata;
* document categorization;
* document versioning;
* document sharing;
* document retention.

Not responsible for:

* document content interpretation;
* financial calculations;
* AI explanations.

---

# 3. Business Concepts

## Document

A digital artifact associated with one Workspace.

Examples:

* Bank Statement
* Tax Certificate
* Portfolio Report
* Contract
* Property Deed
* Investment Agreement
* User Uploaded File

---

## Document Category

Logical classification.

Examples:

* Financial
* Legal
* Tax
* Personal
* Generated

---

## Document Version

Historical revision of a document.

---

## Metadata

Business information describing a document.

Examples:

* Title
* Category
* Date
* Provider
* Related Portfolio
* Related Asset

---

# 5. Business Rules

### BR-001

Every Document belongs to exactly one Workspace.

### BR-002

Documents never disappear permanently through business operations.

### BR-003

Document history is preserved.

### BR-004

Metadata may change independently of document content.

### BR-005

Generated documents are distinguishable from uploaded documents.

* Access follows Workspace permissions.
* Sensitive documents require authorization.
* Downloads are auditable.

---

# 9. AI Considerations

AI may:

* summarize documents;
* classify documents;
* extract metadata.

AI must never:

* modify document contents;
* fabricate document information.
